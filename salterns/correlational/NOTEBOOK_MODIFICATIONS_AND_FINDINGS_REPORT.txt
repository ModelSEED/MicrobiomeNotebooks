SECTION 3: EXPECTED DISCREPANCIES BETWEEN MODEL AND LITERATURE
================================================================================

Based on typical limitations of genome-scale metabolic models and gaps in
genome annotations, we anticipate several categories of discrepancies:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 CATEGORY 1: Missing Gene Annotations (FALSE NEGATIVES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Problem:**
Model lacks reactions despite organism having capability experimentally

**Expected Cases:**

A. BETAINE REDUCTASE COMPLEX (GrdHI)
- Gene names: grdH, grdI (subunits), grdA (selenocysteine protein)
- Reaction: rxs17220_c0 may be absent
- Impact: Cannot grow on betaine despite literature evidence
- Affected: Likely 2-4 models in Halanaerobiales or Peptostreptococcales

B. SELENOCYSTEINE INCORPORATION MACHINERY
- Genes: selA, selB, selC (tRNA), selD (selenophosphate synthetase)
- Impact: Cannot use selenite even if betaine reductase genes present
- Affected: Most Peptostreptococcales models
- Manifestation: No growth enhancement with selenite addition

C. TMA TRANSPORT
- Gene: TMA transporter (various families)
- Reaction: EX_cpd00441_e0 or rxs00002_c0 may be absent
- Impact: TMA accumulates intracellularly, becomes toxic
- Affected: 3-5 models across multiple orders

D. AMINO ACID TRANSPORTERS
- Genes: Multiple ABC transporters, permeases
- Impact: Cannot import amino acids for Stickland reaction
- Affected: Synergistota models primarily

E. THIOREDOXIN SYSTEM
- Genes: trxA (thioredoxin), trxB (thioredoxin reductase)
- Reaction: rxn27318_c0 may be absent
- Impact: Cannot regenerate reduced thioredoxin for betaine reductase
- Affected: 2-3 models

**Detection in Results:**
- No growth on media where growth expected (HIGH confidence)
- has_betaine_reductase = False in model with expected capability
- Discrepancy: "Expected growth on betaine minimal (HIGH CONFIDENCE) - NO GROWTH"

**Recommendation:**
- Manual genome re-annotation with HMM profiles for grd genes
- Check for distant homologs using PSI-BLAST
- Examine genomic context (gene clusters) for pathway genes

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2 CATEGORY 2: Incomplete Metabolic Networks (GAPS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Problem:**
Core pathway present but connecting reactions missing

**Expected Cases:**

A. COFACTOR REGENERATION
- Present: Betaine reductase
- Missing: NADPH regeneration, electron transfer chain components
- Impact: Single-step reaction possible but unsustainable growth
- Manifestation: Very low growth rate (0.001-0.01) instead of expected (>0.05)

B. ACETYL-PHOSPHATE UTILIZATION
- Present: Betaine → Acetyl-phosphate
- Missing: Acetyl-phosphate → Acetyl-CoA → TCA cycle
- Impact: Cannot generate ATP from acetate product
- Manifestation: Betaine uptake without corresponding ATP production

C. TMA METABOLISM
- Present: TMA production
- Missing: TMA dehydrogenase or TMA methyltransferase
- Impact: TMA accumulation (may be okay if exported)
- Manifestation: High TMA:Acetate ratio

D. STICKLAND REACTION COUPLING
- Present: Betaine reductase, amino acid degradation
- Missing: Electron transfer coupling components
- Impact: Cannot couple AA oxidation with betaine reduction
- Manifestation: Growth on amino acids OR betaine, but not enhanced with both

**Detection in Results:**
- Growth feasible but rate << expected
- Abnormal stoichiometry (betaine:TMA:acetate ratios off)
- No synergistic effect in Stickland condition

**Recommendation:**
- Gap-filling analysis targeting cofactor regeneration
- Manual curation of electron transfer pathways
- Validation of gene-protein-reaction associations

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 CATEGORY 3: Incorrect Gene Annotations (FALSE POSITIVES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Problem:**
Model contains reactions not actually present in organism

**Expected Cases:**

A. MISANNOTATED REDUCTASES
- Annotated as: Betaine reductase
- Actually: Glycine reductase, sarcosine reductase (related but different)
- Impact: Model predicts growth but organism cannot actually use betaine
- Affected: 1-2 models, especially in Tissierellales (unclear evidence)

B. PROMISCUOUS ENZYME ANNOTATIONS
- Enzyme annotated with broad substrate specificity
- May not actually accept betaine as substrate
- Impact: Model allows betaine metabolism via alternative pathway

C. TRANSPORTED METABOLITE SPECIFICITY
- Transporter annotated as general quaternary amine transporter
- May not actually transport betaine
- Impact: Betaine can enter cell in model but not in vivo

**Detection in Results:**
- Tissierellales models showing strong betaine growth (LOW expected confidence)
- Growth without expected pathway components
- Discrepancy: Unexpected betaine metabolism in organisms lacking evidence

**Recommendation:**
- Biochemical validation of predicted enzyme activities
- Substrate specificity testing for key reactions
- Literature-based curation of gene annotations

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4 CATEGORY 4: Media Composition Issues
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Problem:**
Defined media missing essential cofactors or trace nutrients

**Expected Cases:**

A. TRACE METALS
- Defined: Zn, Cu, Ca, Co, Ni, Mn, Fe
- Potentially missing: Mo, W (molybdenum/tungsten for some oxidoreductases)
- Impact: Growth lower than expected

- Validate metal cofactor concentrations

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5 CATEGORY 5: Thermodynamic Infeasibility
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Problem:**
Pathway thermodynamically unfavorable under defined conditions

**Expected Cases:**

A. REDOX IMBALANCE
- Betaine reduction requires reduced cofactors
- Insufficient electron donors in minimal medium
- Impact: Thermodynamically infeasible
- Manifestation: Model predicts no growth (correctly)

B. ATP YIELD TOO LOW
- Pathway produces insufficient ATP for maintenance
- ATPM requirement (1-10 mmol/gDW/hr) exceeds production
- Impact: Cannot sustain growth
- Manifestation: Growth only if ATPM lowered

C. PRODUCT INHIBITION
- TMA or acetate accumulation inhibits forward flux
- No export mechanisms
- Impact: Pathway shuts down after initial flux

**Detection in Results:**
- No feasible solution in linear programming
- Growth only under unrealistic flux distributions

**Recommendation:**
- Thermodynamic analysis using ΔGΔR values
- Validation of ATP maintenance requirements
- Check for product export reactions
